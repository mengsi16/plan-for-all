### Audit Notes

- Record high-risk terminology here even if the term looks familiar at first glance.
- Prefer official docs, official repos, official release notes, and official changelogs.
- If no official source exists, use recent high-quality sources and record why they were accepted.
- If a term or claim remains unresolved, state the blocker explicitly and mirror it into `task_plan.md`.
- Record the current task-specific meaning when a term could be misunderstood.
- When new suspicious terminology appears in any later phase, append it here instead of assuming the earlier audit was enough.
- If execution evidence conflicts with a previous audit result, change the status to `stale_recheck_required` or `unresolved`.

## Audit State Guide

- `new`: observed but not yet classified
- `needs_verification`: must be checked before planning or execution depends on it
- `high_risk_terminology`: likely semantic ambiguity or drift; current task meaning must be fixed explicitly
- `verified_official`: verified against authoritative official material
- `verified_recent`: verified using recent high-quality sources when official material was unavailable or insufficient
- `project_specific_ask_user`: meaning belongs to project context or the user's private terminology
- `unresolved`: still uncertain and must remain visible as a blocker or constraint
- `stale_recheck_required`: previously checked, but later phase or evidence requires re-verification
